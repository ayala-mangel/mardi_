How to get there?

From Israel fly to New York and from there on a direct flight to Louis Armstrong Airport in Louisiana. From the airport there are buses and taxis to New Orleans with high frequency. You can also get there by rental car or bus from Chicago (1,500 kilometers), Dallas (800 kilometers), Los Angeles (3,080 kilometers), Miami (1,500 kilometers) and New York (2,130 kilometers). Where do you live?

During the carnival, many streets are closed to traffic (mainly in the French Quarter), so it is recommended to choose the hotels closest to the center of the action and thus avoid the need to travel by public transport. Some hotels offer special packages for the occasion. The Maison Dupuy is two blocks from Bourbon Street, one of the main centers of the festivities. The hotel offers first-class accommodation, in well-equipped rooms and with a variety of treats. Price: from 99 dollars for a double room. Address: 1001 Rue Toulouse. Phone: +1-504-5868000. The Dauphine Orleans Hotel is in the heart of the French Quarter and offers 111 well-equipped and decorated guest rooms. Price: from 149 dollars for a double room. Address: 415 Dauphine St.. Phone: 1-800-5217111. Provincial is a special hotel in the heart of the French Quarter, built around a courtyard with two pools and a patio of tropical vegetation. There are 94 rooms with air conditioning, TV, hairdryer and antique furniture. price: Price: from 119 dollars for a double room. Address: 1024 Rue Chartres. Phone: +1-504-5814995.

Where do you eat?

It is advisable to reserve a place in the restaurants in advance, because during the carnival they are full to the brim. Alternatively, you can make do with the food stalls scattered in the streets or make your own sandwiches. Some of the restaurants on the carnival route offer a package that includes a meal and a place to watch the parades. Michaul's Restaurant offers a meal, alcoholic drink and a viewing spot, from $95 per person. Address: 840 St. Charles Ave. Phone: +1-504-5225517. Cannon's Restaurant is at the starting point of the processions and offers a buffet meal and a viewing spot for $50 per person. Address: 4141 St. Charles Ave. Phone: +1-504-8913200. Palace Cafe is a Cajun restaurant located at the entrance to the French Quarter. In preparation for Mardi Gras, a special menu and places to observe the processions are offered. Address: 605 Canal St.. Phone: +1-504-5231661/5218310. More info New Orleans Tourist Information Office - Address: 1520 Sugar Bowl Drive. Phone: +1-504-5665011. Fax: +1-504-5665021.
